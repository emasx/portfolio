About

I’m a web designer and developer passionate about creating elegant, high-performing websites. I combine creativity, precision, and strategic thinking to craft digital experiences that reflect a brand’s unique identity and deliver real value.

I wrote my first line of code with Codecademy 10 years ago — and from that day, I was hooked. The moment I enrolled in my first course and finished it, I thought: “Wow, I can create anything I want using the right technology.” That realization still drives everything I do today.

Besides design and development, I’m also an English teacher. I’ve been teaching for five years since graduating from the Faculty of Letters, and I deeply believe in the power of education — the only true weapon that can help you achieve anything you dream of.

“Education is not the learning of facts, but the training of the mind to think.” — Albert Einstein

My approach is built on clarity, simplicity, and timeless design — every project starts with understanding people, not pixels. I love collaborating with brands and entrepreneurs who value design as a tool for growth and connection.

Burgerlicious

Mobile App UI
Overview

Burgerlicious is a clean, modern food-ordering mobile app concept designed entirely in Figma. The project showcases a complete user flow—from splash screen to login, browsing menu categories, and viewing detailed food items. The goal was to create a visually appealing and intuitive interface that feels familiar and smooth for users ordering food on the go.


What I Focused On

Throughout this project, I strengthened my core Figma skills and applied professional UI/UX design practices:

  • Auto-Layout:

    Used extensively to ensure consistent spacing, easy scalability, and fully responsive frames that adapt smoothly during design changes.

  • Components & Variants:

    Created reusable UI elements such as buttons, input fields, cards, icons, and navigation bars. Variants allowed seamless state changes (active/inactive, filled/outlined buttons, input focus states, etc.).

  • Design System Thinking:

    Built a small but structured style guide—including typography, color styles, and component sets—to maintain visual consistency across the entire app.

  • Prototyping:

    Connected screens with interactive flows to simulate realistic app navigation (Sign-in flow, category browsing, tab bar interactions). Added micro-interactions and transitions to enhance usability.
